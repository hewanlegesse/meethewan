My Travel History

Abu Dhabi, UAE

I spent nearly four years in Abu Dhabi and greatly appreciated the country's hospitality, cultural diversity, and strong sense of safety. The variety of food is impressive, and there are countless places to explore. While the summers are extremely hot, the winter months can get surprisingly cold. Their currency is Dirham.

Nairobo, Kenya

I visited Nairobi for a three-day meeting, and I quickly fell in love with the city. It felt like home — the people were welcoming, and I had the chance to explore some beautiful places. Their currency is the Kenyan shilling

Kigali, Rwanda

I have stayed in Kigali for a week, Kigali left a lasting impression on me — it’s one of the cleanest, calmest, and most organized cities I’ve visited in Africa. The people are kind and respectful, the streets are peaceful, and there’s a strong sense of safety. I especially admired how the city balances modern development with deep cultural roots. It’s a place I’d love to return to

Windhoek, Namibia

I was in Windhoek, Namibia for a week to attend a workshop, and it turned out to be a truly memorable experience. The city is peaceful, clean, and surrounded by stunning natural scenery. I appreciated the blend of modern life with cultural heritage, and the people were warm and welcoming. Though my stay was short, I left with a deep sense of calm and admiration for the country.

Bangkonk, Thailand

I was in Bangkok, Thailand for a one-week training, and the experience was both enriching and enjoyable. The city is full of energy, culture, and color — from its flavorful street food to its beautiful temples and lively markets. The people were welcoming, and I appreciated the mix of tradition and modernity throughout the city. Even with a busy schedule, I managed to explore a few places, and I’d love to go back and see more.
